Brick Executive Search

Digital Marketing Internship

Overview

Redesigned the Brick Executive Search homepage to elevate brand perception, streamline user navigation, and drive client engagement. As the UX/UI Designer, I led the end-to-end design process-conducting research, developing a modern visual identity, optimizing site structure, and enhancing content presentation to align with business goals and marketing strategy.

Goal

The primary goal of the redesign was to create a clean, modern, and user-friendly homepage that clearly communicates Brick Executive Searchâ€™s value proposition while improving navigation and engagement. This aimed to enhance the firm's online presence and attract both potential clients and job candidates.

Role

UX/UI Designer

Tools

Figma

Project Type

Website Redesign

My Role

  • Analyze the usability of the existing Berkshire Hathaway website and propose a redesigned interface that prioritizes clarity, accessibility, and a modern aesthetic.
  • Streamline navigation, simplify tasks like finding financial reports and shareholder letters, and address key pain points identified through heuristic evaluation and usability testing.
  • Design solutions for specific issues such as the lack of error messages, limited search functionality, and the absence of a help/FAQ section to enhance overall user satisfaction.

Create Personas & Usability Test Plan

  • Develop user personas with specific goals and challenges when accessing the Berkshire Hathaway website and a test plan for testing its usability.
  • Participants performed tasks (finding annual reports, shareholder letters) and rated satisfaction (1-5 scale) based on specific usability criteria.

Usability Testing

A mixed-methods approach was employed, leveraging both quantitative and qualitative metrics to comprehensively evaluate the Berkshire Hathaway website's usability.

  • Quantitative Metrics: Task success rates, completion times, and satisfaction scores (using a 5-point Likert scale) were collected to measure task efficiency and user satisfaction.
  • Qualitative Metrics: User interviews and observational analysis were conducted to gain deeper insights into user behaviors, pain points, and overall perceptions of the interface.

Focus

  • How easily users could locate and access key information, such as annual reports and shareholder letters.
  • The effectiveness of the website's navigation menu, search functionality, and overall information organization.
  • The speed and ease with which users could complete specific tasks related to their goals (ex: downloading a financial report, finding a sustainability partner).
  • Overall user perceptions of the website's design, usability, and trustworthiness.

Wireframes

Using Figma, I translated my first sketches into low-fidelity wireframes. The wireframes focused on strategic content placement, readability, and intuitive navigation to support the project's goals.

  • I implemented a reading structure that guides users naturally from the top navigation bar, downwards.
  • The wireframes included dedicated sections for company documents and news letters.
  • New design features a minimalist design with streamlined navigation for easier access.

Lessons Learned

  • Find the right balance between modern design principles and preserving a brand's established identity.
  • Cater to diverse user groups (shareholders, employees, students) by addressing their specific needs and pain points.
  • Successfully update a brand's digital presence by updating typography, color schemes, and adding features to improve accessibility.

Next Steps

  • Create a fully interactive prototype using tools like Figma to test the website's functionality, incorporating the recommended error handling, help/FAQ section, and search filter enhancements.
  • Perform further usability testing with users who fit the created personas and ask them to perform the same tasks to check if those usability changes did improve their experience on the site.
  • Create detailed design specifications, including style guides and interaction details, to provide clear guidance for developers during implementation.
  • Focus on documenting the improved error handling, help/FAQ functionality, and search filter implementations.
