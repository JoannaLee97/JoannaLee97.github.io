Campfire Travel

CMST 295 Fundamentals of Digial Design

Overview

I was tasked with creating a storyboard to transform Campfire Travel, a digital magazine for campers and RVers, into a fully functional website. The project aimed to showcase attractions in California, Oregon, Idaho, and Wyoming, while providing RV setup tips, on-the-go meal ideas, and entertaining travel videos. My responsibilities included leading the design process, developing user personas, creating wireframes, and conducting user research to ensure the website would effectively cater to adventure-seeking travelers looking for both information and convenience.

Goal

SMART goals of the project:

  • Content Discoverability: The layout was designed to showcase 30 in-depth articles on states and events, aiming to increase site traffic by December 2025.
  • Video Integration: Space was allocated for 15 video tutorials on topics like RV setup and outdoor cooking, targeting 100,000 video views by Q3 2026.
  • User Engagement: The clean, intuitive design aimed to attract 2,000 or more unique page views per article within one month of publishing.

Role

Student project

Tools

Figma

Class

Project 2 CMST 295

My Role

  • Using Figma, I transformed initial hand-drawn sketches into low-fidelity wireframes, focusing on strategic content placement, clear readability, and intuitive navigation to support the project’s goals.
  • I structured the wireframes with a logical reading flow from the top navigation downward and included dedicated sections for California, Oregon, Wyoming, and Idaho, each featuring highlighted destinations to engage users.
  • To promote exploration and increase engagement, I incorporated clickable components throughout the wireframes, ensuring users could easily navigate and interact with key site areas.

Lessons Learned

  • Implementing a clear visual hierarchy and organizing content into logical categories significantly improving usability and user engagement.
  • Incorporating interactive features such as video tutorials and clickable elements increased user engagement. This interactive design encouraged website exploration.

Next Steps

  • Create a fully interactive prototype using tools like Figma to test the website's functionality.
  • Organize user testing sessions with diverse group of campers and RVers to gather feedback on the prototype to identify usability issues.
  • Ensure the website is a fully responsive across all devices.
